Hi, my name is Jacob Wolniewicz!

I'm a developer that tries to build software for a better tomorrow.

Mountain addict - brewer - tin foil hat computer scientist - board game geek - instrument tinkerer.

I am a software engineer who started at Microsoft where I learned practices to build software that lasts. Now I work as a tech lead at different companies around Boulder Colorado. When not working I volunteer in the outdoor industry, make beer, play games, and fiddle around with instruments. Currently bagpipes and the piano are my focus.

I sit and think about data and how humans interact with computers.

Every now and then I publish a blog post about recent interactions or thoughts I've had. Please look at my Medium Blog for more.

I'm always happy to hear new thoughts, and talk to others about accessibility. Please feel free to contact me at my email jacob @ wolniewicz dot com if you want to discuss computers and accessibility.

I am passionate about the outdoors, their protection, and teaching others safe techniques to travel. In 2017 through the end of 2018 I was the chair of the Seattle Mountaineers climbing committee. I have been the head instructor on courses around multi pitch trad climbing, glacier travel, and intro to rock climbing courses. I have also been an assistant instructor for AIARE 1 at the Mountaineers. I currently am leading a course on Multi Pitch Trad climbing scheduled for May 2018.

Now that I live in Boulder I have began helping the Colorado Mountain Club on their climbing courses.

Growing up in Colorado I would hike 14ers and camp. When I moved to Washington I wanted to keep getting up peaks but quickly learned that the higher gradiant of their peaks demanded I learn how to climb. Hence my outdoor climbing career began.
